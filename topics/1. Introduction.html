Introduction to ChameleonMap

ChameleonMap stands as a pioneering open-source system, the result of a collaborative effort between the Informatics Institute of the Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul (INF-UFRGS) and the Brazilian National Research Network (RNP). This dynamic system takes its name from its remarkable ability to adapt and transform much like a chameleon, making it an invaluable tool for the creation of interactive maps with geolocated databases.

One of ChameleonMap's standout features is its incredible flexibility and adaptability, making it applicable across a diverse range of domains and contexts. From computer networks to economic landscapes, ChameleonMap offers a versatile platform that can be tailored to suit various needs.

The collaboration between INF-UFRGS and RNP has resulted in a system that goes beyond conventional mapping tools. ChameleonMap not only excels in its technical capabilities but also distinguishes itself through user-friendliness. This quality extends to both system administrators and end-users, making it accessible and efficient for all involved parties.
