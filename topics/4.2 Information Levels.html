Main Features

Information Levels

In ChameleonMap, each registered element (tag, location, or link) can have information assigned at different levels of detail: concise information and detailed information.

Concise Information: This level is displayed in a simple popup of the element. The concise information provides a quick overview with spatial and operational limitations, making it ideal for presenting synthetic information.

Detailed Information: Users can access detailed information in an expanded popup, offering greater representational power. This expanded popup can showcase texts, images, videos, and even incorporate external web pages, making it a powerful tool for representing attributes and data of an element.

Figure 4.2.0 illustrates the two levels of information assigned to a location. On the left side, concise information is displayed within the simple popup. On the right side, the expanded popup shows detailed information, including an image, a table, and formatted text.

When the registered element is a tag, there is an additional intermediate level of representation: the side popup.
